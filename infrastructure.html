Why height matters.

Most tunnels in Europe are 12–17m. Ours is 35m. That means you can do manoeuvres that simply aren't possible in shorter tunnels. It's the difference between a paddling pool and the ocean.

At 35m, you can fly a full set without being interrupted by the net or the top. You can actually accelerate, transition, and recover — all in one continuous pass. That's what makes Luxfly different. That's why teams come from Belgium, France, and Germany to train here.

Engineering

Four motors. Seamless airflow.

The Luxfly tunnel uses four synchronised electric motors mounted above the recirculation columns. The air enters from below, rises through the flying chamber, folds through the return ducts, and re-enters at the base.

This closed-loop system means constant, controllable airspeed with no cold draughts and no dead zones. The control system allows the tunnel operator to adjust wind speed in real-time to suit the flyer's level.

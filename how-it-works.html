Step by Step

From the door to the sky.

Here's what to expect from the moment you arrive to the moment you land.

Your first flight in four steps

01

Arrive & Check In

Show up 15 minutes before your session. Bring ID. Reception will check you in and pair you with your instructor. If you're flying with a group, this is where you meet everyone. We'll answer any last-minute questions and make sure you're comfortable.

02

Gear Up

Helmet, flight suit, goggles, earplugs. All included and provided by us. The gear room is right next to the tunnel. Your instructor fits everything to you personally, adjusting each piece until it feels right. Nothing stays on if it doesn't fit correctly.

03

Safety Briefing

A 10-15 minute session where your instructor walks you through the basic positions, hand signals, and what to expect inside the tunnel. No prior experience needed — that's what the briefing is for. You'll learn how to hold your body, how to communicate, and exactly what's going to happen.

04

You Fly

1-2 minute sessions (depending on your package) with your instructor inside the tunnel with you the whole time. After each session you debrief, rest, and go again. The instructor will be right there, making adjustments and building your confidence with every minute in the air.

"The second you're in the air, fear becomes laughter."
First time?

You'll feel nervous. That's normal.

Most first-timers feel the nerves before they go in. The second you're in the air, nerves become laughter. The sensation of weightlessness hits immediately and overrides any anxiety. By the time you land, you're already planning when you'll come back.

Instructors are trained to get you comfortable fast. They read your body language, they know exactly what first-timers are thinking, and they've guided hundreds of nervous people into the air successfully. Everyone flies eventually.

By your second session you'll be asking for more time. By your third, you'll understand why people get addicted to this.

What you need to know

What to wear

Wear comfortable, close-fitting clothes. Jeans, leggings, tracksuit bottoms all work. Hair should be tied back if longer than shoulder-length. Wear lace-up shoes or trainers.

Don't wear:

  • Scarves or loose neckwear
  • Loose jewellery or accessories
  • Watches or bulky rings
  • Glasses (contacts are fine)

What not to bring

  • Phones or cameras (prohibited in tunnel)
  • Loose keys or small items
  • Food or drink into the facility
  • Anything of irreplaceable sentimental value

We provide secure lockers for your valuables. Professional photography is available if you want photos of your flight.

Health notes

You must be in good general health to fly. Those with back or neck issues, heart conditions, or who are pregnant should consult a doctor before booking.

Recent surgery, uncontrolled epilepsy, or severe vertigo are contraindications. If you're unsure, contact us — we can advise on a case-by-case basis. Full medical conditions are listed in our terms.

Quick answers

How long is a session?

Typically 1-2 minutes of air time, plus briefing and gear-up. Total experience time is about 45 minutes to 1 hour depending on your package. Expect to spend 10-15 minutes gearing up and another 10-15 minutes briefing before you enter the tunnel.

Can I bring my phone in?

No. Phones cannot go inside the tunnel — too much wind and too much risk of losing it. We have a viewing area where your friends and family can watch. Professional photography packages are available if you want images of your flight.

What if I'm scared?

Tell your instructor. They'll take it slow. Most instructors recommend starting with a shorter session if you're nervous. Everyone flies eventually once they feel the air supporting them. Fear is natural — confidence comes after the first flight.

Is it loud?

Yes — the motors are powerful. Earplugs are included and mandatory. Inside the tunnel, communication happens by hand signals because you can't hear speech. Outside the tunnel, debriefing is perfectly audible.

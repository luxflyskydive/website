The Activity

What is indoor skydiving?

A wind tunnel 35 metres tall. Up to 350 km/h of smooth vertical airflow. And you, weightless inside it.

Europe's Tallest

Not a simulation. The real thing.

Indoor skydiving is freefall. Pure and simple. You step into a vertical wind tunnel where air moves at speeds up to 350 km/h. That airflow is strong enough to support your body weight, creating the exact sensation of jumping from an aeroplane. The difference: you're indoors, the height is controlled, and there's no parachute needed because the air keeps you aloft.

The sensation hits you immediately. The rush of wind across your face. Your body becoming weightless. The moment you stop fighting the air and start flying. Instructors are with you the entire time, guiding every movement, making sure you're safe and having the time of your life.

Most people think indoor skydiving is a simulation. It's not. It's the real physics of freefall, just in a controlled environment. Pro skydivers train in wind tunnels like ours. Commercial spaceflight companies use wind tunnels to prepare astronauts. This is how humans learn to fly.

The Feeling

Nothing else feels like this.

Your body floats. Your mind goes quiet. The only thing you feel is the rush of wind on your face and the sensation of weightlessness that most people experience only once in their lifetime — in a skydive. But here, you can have that feeling again and again.

The moment you're in the air, fear becomes laughter. Your instructor is right there with you, holding you steady, encouraging every movement. By your second flight, you're asking for more time. By your third, you're booking your next session. This is addictive in the best way possible.

Indoor vs. outdoor skydiving

Indoor

  • No aeroplane needed
  • Weather-independent
  • Completely controlled environment
  • Repeatable experience
  • Anyone can do it
  • Immediate feedback from instructor

Outdoor

  • Requires aeroplane and jump pilot
  • Weather dependent
  • Single freefall experience
  • Requires certification
  • Limited by physical fitness
  • Feedback comes after landing

Did you know? Many professional skydivers train in wind tunnels like ours before attempting outdoor freefall. Indoor flying teaches body awareness, stability, and control that translates directly to the sky. You'll actually develop better flying skills in a tunnel than you ever could jumping from a plane once or twice a year.

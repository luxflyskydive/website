Pro Flying

This isn't just a sport. It's a way of life.

Indoor skydiving is a recognised competitive sport with national and international championships. Luxfly is where Luxembourg's pro flyers train.

Beyond the first flight.

Indoor skydiving at a recreational level is one thing. You learn to fall safely, to understand your body in air, to experience the freedom of weightlessness. But competitive wind sport is a different world entirely.

Body flying disciplines — formation skydiving, freestyle, speed, vertical — each require hundreds of hours of deliberate practice. Every manoeuvre has a precise technique. Every transition must be smooth, controlled, repeatable.

The wind tunnel makes that practice accessible. No weather delays. No altitude constraints. No waiting between jumps. You can log 100 hours in a season instead of 100 jumps.

Disciplines of competitive flying

Formation (FS)

2 or 4-way synchronised formations built in the sky.

Freestyle

Artistic solo flying judged on creativity and body control.

Speed

Fastest tunnel spin time. Pure acceleration and control.

Canopy Piloting

Advanced open canopy flying. Beyond the tunnel.

Training

Consistency is everything.

Pro flyers typically train 2–4 hours per week in the tunnel. That's 100–200 hours per year. At that volume, progression is measurable month to month.

At Luxfly, pro booking packages mean you can plan your training calendar months in advance. The tunnel is yours — no interruptions, no tourists, no crowding. Just work.

Serious flyers come to Luxfly because the tunnel is the best in Europe. 35 metres. That changes everything.

Where Luxfly flyers compete

Members of the Luxfly pro programme have competed at the Belgian Open, German Nationals, and the FAI Skydiving World Cup. We support Luxembourg's competitive flyers with preferential training rates and dedicated coaching slots.
